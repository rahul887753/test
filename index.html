BIS Scheme-I ( ISI mark) for forign manufacturers( FMCS)
"The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) Scheme-I, denoted by the Indian Standards Institution (ISI) Mark, is a statutory certification program that ensures products conform to the applicable Indian Standards. International manufacturers seeking to export products to India are required to obtain ISI certification, for the products which are notified in the mandatory product list to demonstrates compliance with Indian regulatory requirements. The certification process involves a comprehensive evaluation, comprising product testing, inspection, assessment of quality assurance team to ensure consistent adherence to prescribed standards for quality, safety, and performance. Successful certification enables international manufacturers to access the Indian market, while instilling confidence among stakeholders, including consumers and regulators, regarding the product's conformity to established standards.
BIS Scheme-I( ISI mark) for Domestic manufactures
"The BIS Scheme-I, denoted by the ISI Mark, is a certification scheme administered by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) to ensure conformity of products to the applicable Indian Standards. To obtain ISI certification, manufacturers must undergo a rigorous process, which includes product testing, inspection, assessment of quality control team competency, and regular surveillance. This comprehensive evaluation ensures that products bearing the ISI Mark consistently meet the relevant standards for quality, safety, and performance. The ISI Mark serves as a guarantee of product conformity, providing assurance to consumers, regulators, and other stakeholders that the product adheres to established standards."
BIS Scheme-II Compulsory Registration Scheme
The BIS Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS) is a mandatory certification program for the products fall under the CRS scheme. It is governed by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS). Manufacturers, importers, and sellers of notified products are required to register their products with BIS and obtain a unique registration number. The registration process involves submission of requisite documents, self-declaration, and verification, followed by regular surveillance and audit to ensure continued compliance with the applicable Indian Standards. The scheme aims to ensure that products conform to the essential safety and performance requirements, thereby protecting consumer interests and promoting public health and safety. Compliance with the BIS CRS is mandatory for manufacturers and importers intending to sell their products in India.
